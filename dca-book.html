Editorial · Print · Creative · 2021

DCA Book & Poster

A book about dollar-cost averaging — a long-term investment strategy that more people should use. The design job was to make a financial concept feel clear, calm, and worth reading.

Client
Independent / Personal Project
Role
Editorial Designer
Timeline
2021
Deliverables
Book Design · Cover · Layout · Poster
01 — Challenge

Finance that feels human.

Most finance books look like finance books — dense, serious, a little intimidating. This one needed to feel different: accessible enough for a first-time investor, but thoughtful enough that an experienced reader wouldn't feel talked down to.

The design challenge was finding a visual language that made numbers and strategy feel interesting and approachable, without dumbing anything down.

02 — Approach

Clear thinking. Well designed.

01

Book Cover

A cover that communicated the core idea — steady, consistent growth — without relying on stock market imagery or corporate clichés.

02

Interior Layout

A typographic layout that gave the content room to breathe — clear hierarchy, good reading rhythm, and visual moments that broke up the text without distracting from it.

03

Data Visualisation

Charts and diagrams designed to illustrate the strategy clearly — no unnecessary complexity, just the information the reader needed.

04

Companion Poster

A poster version of the core concept — designed as a shareable, displayable piece that could live on a wall or a social feed.

03 — Outcomes
1
Book + poster system from a single visual concept
Print
Designed for print with digital-sharing in mind
Clear
Financial content made genuinely readable
