## PIECE 1 — LEAD (top of page)

*Replaces the current `.cs-lead` text. Client's brand story, retold for easy reading. Keep as 3 short paragraphs; the philosophy name "Minimal Contrast" must stay.*

The quietest experiences often mean the most.

Ordinary Bangkok begins with light and shadow. What looks plain at first slowly reveals its depth, its character, and its intent. Every detail is refined with care, yet none of it asks for attention. The identity simply invites a second look — and rewards it with soft textures, gentle contrasts, and balanced proportions.

This philosophy is called Minimal Contrast. Less becomes more — not through decoration, but through precision.

## PIECE 2 — ABOUT THE PROJECT (section 01 — Overview)

*One short heading + two short paragraphs. The two logo images (detail sheet, then clear-space diagram) follow directly after this text.*

### Heading

The ordinary, made precise

### Body

Ordinary Bangkok is a Thai homeware label. Its trays and bowls are turned from ash, oak, walnut, and teak, and they are deliberately quiet objects. The identity had to match: nothing loud, nothing extra — "creatively ordinary." So the whole brand rests on one gentle move. The wordmark is a single line of lowercase serif type, with "ordinary" in soft grey and "bangkok" in near-black. That small step in tone is the logo. It is Minimal Contrast at work.

A mark this simple leaves nowhere to hide, so the craft sits in the details. The drawings below show how tight the system is. The first refines the letterforms down to the curve of a single terminal. The second protects the mark with its own alphabet: the "o" of ordinary sets the clear space on every side.

## PIECE 3 — GRAPHIC STYLE (section body)

### Headline (fixed by client — use exactly, with line break at "/")

Nothing is added. / Only perception is refined.

### Body

The graphic style follows a Thai idea: สัจจะวัตถุ — truth to materials. The object is shown as it is. Each white sleeve carries one grayscale photograph of the piece inside — stan, mani, marin — with no props, no styling, no color. Slide the kraft drawer open and the photograph gives way to the real thing. The two match.

Everything else stays plain so the wood can speak. The box is raw kraft. The type is small and factual: the product name in lowercase serif, the wood options — ash, oak, walnut — and a few honest lines from the brand. The grain of each tray is the only ornament, and it needs no help.
